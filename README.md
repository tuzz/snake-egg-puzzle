### Snake Egg puzzle

A Sentient program to solve Snake Egg puzzles. The puzzle is explained in [this
YouTube video](https://www.youtube.com/watch?v=73Meh3NPno4).

[Sentient](https://sentient-lang.org/) is a programming language I wrote a few
years ago to solve difficult combinatorial problems. This seemed like a fun
and unusual problem so I thought I'd try using Sentient to solve it. It was
definitely a challenge and required thinking hard about some tricky constraints
but it works!

The example puzzle from the YouTube video is in the `example/` directory. You
can solve this puzzle with Sentient by providing assignments that specify the
head/tail positions of the snake and the two numbers given to you for this
puzzle. Here's how you'd do that:

```sh
sentient --run snake-egg.json --machine lingeling --assign-file example/assignments.json \
         | ruby pretty_print.rb
```

This produces the following output after about two minutes:

```
9 9 █ █ █ 7 █ █ █ █
9 9 9 9 █ 7 █ 5 5 █
█ █ █ 9 █ 7 █ █ 5 █
2 2 █ 9 █ 7 7 █ 5 █
█ █ █ 9 █ 7 █ █ 5 █
█ 8 8 █ █ 7 █ 4 █ █
█ █ 8 █ 1 █ █ 4 █ 6
3 █ 8 █ █ █ 4 4 █ 6
3 █ 8 8 8 8 █ █ █ 6
3 █ █ █ █ █ █ 6 6 6
```

Alternatively, you can create your own assignments to solve different puzzles or
run Sentient with no assignments at all to generate finished puzzles! Check out
the comments in the `snake-egg.snt` file for a general overview of how the
constraints are specified.

There's also a `search_minimal.rb` script that takes, as input, a finished
puzzle and tries to find the minimal sets of numbers that produce it as a unique
solution. This is very time consuming - it iteratively solves the puzzle over
and over.
